Welcome. Let's get you recording.

Komodos records your meetings, transcribes them, and writes the summary — all on your own Mac. Setup takes about two minutes. There's one macOS step that trips people up, so we've spelled it out below.

Download for Mac Apple Silicon · macOS 14.2+ · ~55 MB
Signed & notarized by Apple. Your recordings never leave your Mac.

Set up in four steps

1

Install it

Open the downloaded Komodos‑Desktop‑Recorder.dmg, then drag the Komodos app onto the Applications folder in the window that appears.

2

Open it the first time — macOS will ask you to confirm

The very first time you open Komodos, macOS shows a security prompt. This is normal — macOS does it for every app you download from the web, and it does not mean anything is wrong. You only do this once.

On macOS 15 (Sequoia)

  1. If you see “Apple could not verify…”, click Done — not Move to Trash.
  2. Open menu → System Settings → Privacy & Security.
  3. Scroll to Security. You'll see “Komodos Desktop Recorder was blocked…”
  4. Click Open Anyway, confirm with Touch ID or your password, then Open Anyway once more.

On macOS 14 (Sonoma) it's faster: right‑click (or Control‑click) the app in Applications → Open → Open.

Don't see an “Open Anyway” button? Open Terminal (Applications → Utilities), paste this, press Return, then open the app: xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine "/Applications/Komodos Desktop Recorder.app"

3

Allow microphone & system audio

Komodos lives in your menu bar (near the clock), not the Dock. Click its icon and grant the two prompts macOS shows.

System Audio Recording is the important one — it's what lets Komodos hear the other people on a call, not just you.

4

Press Record

Before a meeting, click the menu‑bar icon and hit Start Recording. Hit Stop when it ends — the transcript and summary appear automatically in your dashboard. The first recording downloads the on‑device model once; after that it works offline.

What stays on your Mac

  • Everything runs locally. Recordings and transcripts stay in a folder on your Mac — nothing is sent to a server you don't control.
  • Cloud AI is opt‑in. Summaries use an on‑device model unless you add your own API key.
  • You control recording. Please tell people when you're recording — it's the right thing to do (and the law in some places).
