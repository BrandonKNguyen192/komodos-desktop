00

What Komodos actually does

Komodos lives in your Mac's menu bar (not the Dock). When you're on a call, it captures two tracks at once — the other people (your Mac's system audio) and you (your microphone) — so the whole conversation gets transcribed, not just your half.

The rhythm you'll fall into is three steps: record → transcribe → ask. You record a meeting, Komodos writes it up on-device with speaker labels and a summary, and then you can ask questions about one meeting or across your entire history — with clickable citations back to the exact moment someone said it.

The privacy part

Everything runs on your Mac by default — recording, transcription, and AI all happen locally. Cloud features (a specific AI model, OneDrive backup, calendar) are clearly labeled and off until you turn them on. There's even an Offline switch that hard-blocks all network access if you want a guarantee.

01

Before you start

A 60-second check so nothing surprises you:

  • Apple Silicon Mac (M1 or newer). Intel Macs aren't supported — don't try to force it, just tell me.
  • macOS 14.2 (Sonoma) or newer. A couple of features (live captions & translation) need macOS 26; everything else works on 14.2+.
  • ~5–8 GB free disk for the on-device AI models it downloads the first time you transcribe.
  • You'll grant Microphone and System Audio Recording permissions on first launch — that's what lets it hear both sides.
02

Install

  1. Download the app

    Open Komodos Desktop Recorder.dmg from the link I sent you.

  2. Drag it to Applications

    In the window that opens, drag the Komodos icon onto the Applications folder.

  3. Open it

    Launch Komodos Desktop Recorder from Applications or Launchpad. Look for the record icon up near the clock — that's home.

One-time approval — this is normal

The app is signed with an Apple Developer ID and notarized by Apple (Apple scanned this exact build and confirmed it's clean). But because you downloaded it from the web instead of the App Store, macOS still asks you to confirm the first time. It does not mean anything is wrong.

On macOS 15 (Sequoia) you'll see "Apple could not verify… is free of malware." Click Done (not Move to Trash), then open System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security, scroll to Security, and click Open Anyway → authenticate → Open Anyway once more. It never asks again.

On macOS 14 (Sonoma) it's quicker: right-click the app in Applications → Open → Open. One time only.

If there's no Open Anyway button, open Terminal and paste this, then open the app normally:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine "/Applications/Komodos Desktop Recorder.app"

If macOS instead says the app is "damaged," don't force it — tell me and I'll re-cut the build.

03

First launch — what to expect

  1. Grant permissions

    Komodos asks for Microphone and System Audio Recording (and Speech Recognition if you'll use live captions). Grant them — it genuinely can't record without them. Missed one? Add it later in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security, or from the menu bar's "Grant Mic + System-Audio Permission" button.

  2. Pick a transcription engine

    You'll get a "Set up transcription" card that recommends one for your Mac's RAM. The default is fine — Qwen3-ASR is most accurate (wants ~18 GB+ RAM), Whisper is lighter and faster on smaller Macs.

  3. Let the first model download

    A one-time model download (a few GB) starts in the background. Your first transcription will be slow while this finishes — that's expected, not a hang. Everything after it is fast.

Tip

Open the dashboard any time from the menu bar → Open Dashboard. That window is where your recordings, transcripts, summaries, notebooks, and settings live.

04

Your first recording

  1. Open the menu-bar panel

    Click the record icon in the top-right menu bar to open Komodos's panel.

  2. Choose what to capture

    Expand Recording source and set Capture to All system audio (every participant) or scope it to just one app — Only Zoom / Teams / Meet / FaceTime. Apps currently making sound show a 🔊. Scope to one app when you don't want music or notifications bleeding in.

  3. Start

    Click Start Recording — or just press ⌘R while the panel is open. A red dot and timer appear.

  4. Confirm both sides are landing

    Watch the floating pill: the MIC and SYS meters should both light up. If only one moves, you're missing a permission — grant it and restart the take.

  5. Stop & transcribe

    Click Stop & Transcribe (the same button, now relabeled) — or press ⌘S. Komodos transcribes on-device; a "Processing on device" bar shows the stages (Transcribing → Aligning → Diarizing).

  6. Read it

    Open the finished recording from the Recordings list to see the transcript, speaker labels, timestamps, and (if auto-summary is on) a summary.

Don't want to save it?

Under Recording source, turn Save recording off. The button becomes Start Live Captions and nothing is written to disk — handy for privately translating a call or video in the moment.

05

How long does transcription take?

It all happens on your Mac, faster than real time — a meeting is ready to read minutes after you stop, not after it plays back start to finish. Two things set the pace:

  • Which engine. Balanced mode — the default — runs Whisper‑turbo, the fast one. High Performance runs Qwen3‑ASR, which is more accurate but roughly 1.5–2× slower. Balanced/Whisper is the default on every Mac; Qwen3‑ASR is the accuracy upgrade you switch on.
  • Which Mac. More GPU cores and memory bandwidth = faster; a fanless MacBook Air also eases off a little under sustained load.

Rough time to transcribe a 1‑hour recording (speaker labeling adds only tens of seconds on top — a bit more on slower Macs):

Your MacWhisper · Balanced (default)Qwen3‑ASR · High Performance
M3 Ultra · 96 GBMEASURED~1.5 min~2.5 min
M5 Pro / Max *~3–5 min~5–8 min
M1 Pro · 16 GB~5–9 min~9–15 min ‡
M4 MacBook Air~5–10 min~12–20 min ‡
M1 MacBook Air~8–15 minstays on Whisper

Only the M3 Ultra row is measured (timed on that exact chip); the rest are estimates that scale with GPU cores (Whisper) and memory bandwidth (Qwen), so treat them as ballpark. * M5 is the newest silicon — projected; a base M5 (no Pro/Max) stays on Whisper like the Airs. ‡ Not the auto‑pick on that Mac: Qwen3‑ASR wants a Pro/Max/Ultra with 18 GB+, so even in High Performance these stay on Whisper — the Qwen figure is if you force it in Settings, and it runs tighter there.

Which should you pick?

Leave it on Balanced (Whisper) for speed and a responsive Mac — it's excellent for the vast majority of meetings. Switch to High Performance (Qwen3‑ASR) when you want the most accurate transcript — dense jargon, strong accents, lots of names — and you're on a Pro/Max/Ultra that's plugged in. One caveat either way: the first‑ever transcription is slower while the model downloads once; every time above is the steady state after that.

07

Make it yours

None of these are required for daily use, but they're where Komodos gets interesting. Most live in Settings in the dashboard; some are in the menu-bar app. A few sync between the two within ~15 seconds.

Privacy & AI

  • Offline / Online mode. Offline is a hard egress guard, not a preference — with it on, backups, calendar, recap email, remote access, and cloud AI all refuse and show an amber "go Online" banner. Recordings are always transcribed locally either way.
  • On-device vs cloud AI. Summaries and chat use the private bundled model by default. You can bring your own key (LM Studio, OpenAI, NVIDIA) under Settings ▸ LLMs — cloud providers show an explicit "your transcripts leave your Mac" warning and are blocked in Offline mode.
  • Performance mode (Balanced · High · Auto) under Settings ▸ Transcription trades transcription speed against keeping the rest of your Mac snappy. Applies to your next recording.

Look & reach

  • 10 themes + accent. The theme icon in the top bar switches between bright-white, warm Paper, Ink, and frosted-glass dark variants, and recolors the accent. Saved per-device in your browser.
  • Remote access over Tailscale. Put your otherwise-offline Mac on your private tailnet so your phone can read notes and record new ones — never exposed to the public internet. Needs the free Tailscale app installed and an explicit "my data will leave this device" acknowledgement.

Don't lose your history

  • Encrypted cloud backups. Connect OneDrive or Google Drive, then Settings ▸ Desktop App ▸ Encrypted DB backup nightly copies an age-encrypted snapshot off your Mac. Online-only; the health card goes red if a backup is stale.

Save your backup recovery key the moment you see the amber nag

Backups are encrypted and can only be decrypted with your recovery key. Click Reveal recovery key → Copy → paste it into your password manager. The key lives only on this Mac — a lost Mac plus an unsaved key means the history is gone for good.

Meetings & automation

  • Auto-detect calls. When your mic and speakers are both live, Komodos pops a one-click "Record this call?" so you never forget. App-agnostic; it deliberately ignores dictation tools like Wispr Flow.
  • Calendar + meeting prep. Connect Outlook or Google Calendar and a "Coming up" panel appears with AI prep briefs drawn from your past recordings. Optional pre-meeting push (via the ntfy app) and auto-recap emails after each recording.
  • Launch at login keeps it always ready in the menu bar. Check for Updates pulls the latest build — DMG testers should use this button, since the plain DMG doesn't self-update.
Good news for beta testers

In this build, all Pro features are unlocked and free — encrypted backup, remote access, and the full Studio. No license key needed.

08

Tips & shortcuts

Worth knowing

  • Grant both Microphone and System Audio Recording. A missing permission silently drops one side of the conversation — the single most common first-run gotcha.
  • The first transcription is slow, then fast forever. That's the one-time model download, not a stuck app.
  • Turn on semantic search (the green dot on the Memory page) before judging Perfect Memory — without it, recall falls back to keyword-only.
  • Don't switch the embedding model mid-demo — it re-indexes your whole history and search is degraded until it finishes.
  • An orange banner on a recording card means that take was cut short (e.g. disk full), not stopped cleanly — so you can tell it apart from a good one.
  • Live captions & translation need macOS 26. On Sonoma/Sequoia the section just says it's unavailable.

09

When something looks wrong

  • "Apple could not verify… free of malware" on install → normal for a notarized web download. Follow the Gatekeeper steps above (Open Anyway). You only do it once.
  • macOS says the app is "damaged" → don't force it. Tell me and I'll re-cut the build.
  • Only one of MIC / SYS lights up → a permission is missing. Grant Microphone and System Audio Recording, then start a new take.
  • The dashboard "Record" button won't record → the menu-bar Mac app isn't running or connected. Open it first; the button nudges you when it can't reach it.
  • First transcription seems stuck → it's the one-time model download finishing in the background. Give it a few minutes; it's fast after that.
  • A cloud feature refuses with an amber banner → you're in Offline mode. Flip to Online (that guard is working as designed).
10

Report a problem

Komodos keeps nothing about you on any server — so if something breaks, I can't see it unless you tell me. When it does:

Help ▸ Report a Problem… (in the menu bar when the app is focused).

It drops a Komodos-report-….zip on your Desktop with app logs and your Mac's specs — no recordings, transcripts, or passwords. Send that zip with a sentence about what happened. That's usually enough to pin the bug. Screenshots are gold.

One responsibility

Komodos records the other people on a call, not just you. Please get their consent before recording — some places legally require it. A good default: say at the start that you're recording. Your recordings stay on your Mac.
